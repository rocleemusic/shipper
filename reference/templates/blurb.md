# Template — Pitch Blurb

The 2–3 sentence submission line. Leads with the hook (Gate 3), names the workflow and the decision, and reads cleanly to a stranger who hasn't opened the repo.

## Skeleton
> [HOOK — one line, the chosen Gate-3 angle.]
> [WHAT IT IS — the workflow it owns + the decision it makes, in one sentence.]
> [WHO IT'S FOR / THE PROOF — who runs it, or the one verifiable fact.]

## Filled example (duel hook)
> Same prospect, two AIs: one writes a confident dossier, the other refuses to — and routes it instead.
> [Name] is a prospect-qualification operator for a game-audio studio: it reads a studio, decides pursue / nurture / decline, and drafts the outreach with the warm intro already found.
> Built by the person who runs that studio's BD — every rule is one he actually uses.

## Bar
- [ ] 2–3 sentences. Hook first, methodology never first.
- [ ] A stranger gets what it is without the repo.
- [ ] One concrete, verifiable detail — no adjectives doing the work.
