# Winner Pattern Catalogue

Distilled from shipped rapid-build competition entries. These are the patterns that separated winners and top mentions from the field. Shipper's gates encode these; this file is the evidence behind them.

## The five patterns that win

1. **Lived expertise beats researched personas.** Every winner built from inside their own world — a café owner's hospitality coach, a son productionizing his father's 25-year physiotherapy practice. "Productionize your opinion." Researched personas lose even when well-built.

2. **Distribution is the tiebreaker.** The sharpest-scoped entry in one week lost the top spot for one reason, stated by the judge: "No video, no landing yet. Add those and this wins outright next time." Same build, plus distribution, wins.

3. **Refusal architecture is the signature move.** Every standout has a named thing it won't do — refuses to cite an unverified source, refuses to act before it knows the angle, refuses to write a dossier. The refusal is the proof it's an operator, not a chatbot.

4. **Auditability converts.** A judge guide with falsifiable prompts ("fire this, expect this behavior"), IDs on load-bearing rules, a committed sample run, logged rejected sources. Anything that lets a stranger verify the claim cold converts directly to placement.

5. **Repeat presence compounds.** Judges name the repeat builders every week. A multi-week portfolio of shipped builds with videos and brand pages closes deals on its own.

## The launch stack (what "ship beyond the repo" means)
- **Table stakes:** clean repo, README with a hook, a worked example output.
- **Winner kit:** + landing page, 60-second short, full walkthrough video.
- **Edge:** + live demo, side-by-side comparison, judge guide, founder story.

## The strongest demo pattern
Same input, two columns: a generic AI beside the operator. The generic one writes a confident paragraph; the operator refuses to, and does the disciplined thing instead. One judge called this "the whole pitch in 30 seconds."
